Krapperup's farm museum

In the early part of the 1900's there were some 50 people employed on the Krapperup estate. Without this workforce, the estate could not keep up with demands. As early as 1951, horses and their equipment was replaced by tractors and new forms of mechanisation. As a result a reduced workforce was needed.

Service in the castle still required a large staff, to carry wood and coke, water, food and deal with waste. Water and heating pipes were installed in teh castle in 1930. This made it possible to reduce the workforce. Life during the early 1900's, when a large family and a lot of guests lived at the castle changed preceptably in the following 50 years.

As an alternative to image transfer by DVD, veterinarians are welcome to send their DICOM images via email as file attachments to, dkk@sund.ku.dk. The section can receive up to 50 MBytes per mail in file attachments (Please note that DICOM images are large, 7 to 16 MBytes is typical). Please send one email per Danish Kennel Club Sags Number (i.e. one set of attachments each for hips, shoulder and elbow evaluations). Do not send multiple images of one region; send only images you wish to be evaluated.

An arrangement with a file sharing service that is in accordance with current EU General Data Protection Regulations is currently under negotiation and details of file transfer via that route will be made available here.
